History of C&S

The Cherubim and Seraphim Church (C&S) is a Christian denomination that originated in Nigeria in the early 20th century. It was founded by Moses Orimolade Tunolase, who claimed to have received a divine revelation to establish a church that would emphasize the power of the Holy Spirit and the importance of spiritual gifts. The church's teachings are rooted in Pentecostalism, and it places a strong emphasis on prayer, healing, and prophecy.
The C&S Church is known for its unique blend of African traditional religious practices and Christian beliefs. It incorporates elements of African spirituality, such as the use of water, oil, and incense in its rituals. The church also emphasizes the importance of holiness and righteousness in the lives of its members.
The C&S Church has a unique liturgy and worship style, which includes the use of hymns, chants, and traditional African music. The church is known for its vibrant worship services, which often involve dancing and clapping. The C&S Church has grown significantly since its founding and has branches in various countries, including the United Kingdom, the United States, and several African nations.
In addition to its spiritual teachings, the C&S Church has also been involved in various social and charitable activities, including education, healthcare, and community development. The church has a strong emphasis on moral values and encourages its members to live a life of holiness and righteousness.
Overall, the C&S Church is a vibrant and dynamic Christian denomination that has made a significant impact on the religious landscape in Nigeria and beyond.
